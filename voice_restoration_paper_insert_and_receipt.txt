Reusable audit prompt.
The manual phrase-search test is only the minimum check. A full reusable prompt for detecting compliance softening, anticipatory hedging, repeated reassurance clauses, false balance, and unnecessary tone dilution is included in the public evidence bundle as:

voice_restoration_anticipatory_compliance_audit_prompt.txt

The prompt asks an AI system to scan a full draft for repeated acknowledgment clauses, softened methodological claims, vague “limitations” language, and unnecessary field-legitimizing caveats. It also instructs the model not to remove caveats that do real evidentiary work: preventing factual overstatement, distinguishing method critique from motive accusation, acknowledging real contrary evidence, defining scope, preserving falsifiability, or clarifying that the critique is about evidence rather than people.

The automated audit should not replace human review. Models can miss context, overcorrect, or fail when the draft exceeds the available context window. Treat the prompt as a screening tool: it finds likely softening patterns so the author can decide whether each caveat is necessary, excessive, or a sign that the model has diluted the argument.
