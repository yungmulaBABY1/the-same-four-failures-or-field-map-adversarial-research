# CONSTRUCT-VALIDITY AUDIT

**Claim:** NP independent practice is clinically equivalent to physician-supervised care across full scope of practice
**Field:** Scope-of-practice legislation, health policy, clinical quality research

-----

## 1. EXACT CLAIM EXTRACTION

**A. Strongest claim made by the source literature itself:**
NPs with ~500 hours of supervised clinical training deliver patient outcomes equivalent to physicians across the full scope of primary care practice, sufficient to justify independent prescribing, diagnosis, and treatment authority without physician oversight.

**B. Weaker defensible claim actually supported by the data:**
NPs working in collaborative/supervised primary care settings produce comparable outcomes to physicians on a narrow set of chronic disease management metrics (HbA1c, blood pressure control, patient satisfaction) in stable, already-diagnosed patient panels.

**C. Inflated claim made by later citations, institutions, and legislative advocacy:**
Training volume is irrelevant to clinical competence; 500 hours is sufficient preparation for independent management of the full undifferentiated scope of primary care, including complex, high-acuity, and diagnostically ambiguous presentations, with no supervision requirement.

**Are A, B, and C the same claim?** No. B is the only claim the data can support. A is an overclaim present in many source papers themselves. C is the legislative and advocacy extrapolation.

**Where does inflation occur?** At two stages: (1) source authors overclaim in conclusions relative to their own methods; (2) advocacy organizations and legislative testimony strip the collaborative-practice context entirely, citing the finding as if it were evidence for unsupervised independent practice.

**Does the source itself overclaim, or is the overclaim mainly downstream?** Both. Source papers frequently conclude “NPs provide equivalent care” without flagging that their samples were NPs under collaborative agreements, not independent practitioners.

-----

## 2. MEASUREMENT EXTRACTION

**What is actually measured:**

- **Sample:** NPs in practice, typically in federally qualified health centers, VA settings, or health systems with collaborative practice agreements in place
- **Population:** Stable, insured or Medicaid primary care patients with established diagnoses (HTN, T2DM, asthma)
- **Instruments:** Administrative claims data, EMR-extracted lab values (HbA1c, BP), patient satisfaction surveys (HCAHPS or CAHPS), readmission rates at 30 days
- **Time scale:** Cross-sectional or short-term longitudinal (1–2 years)
- **Outcome variables:** Surrogate process metrics, not mortality, diagnostic error rates, missed diagnosis, or adverse outcomes in undifferentiated presentations
- **Controls:** Variable; most studies do not adequately control for patient panel complexity, case-mix, or the fact that NPs in collaborative settings can escalate to physicians
- **Exclusions:** Complex, high-acuity, undifferentiated, or rare presentations are structurally excluded from NP panels in collaborative settings — meaning the comparison group is pre-selected for manageable cases
- **What was NOT measured:** Diagnostic accuracy on undifferentiated presentations; missed diagnosis rates; performance on novel, complex, or multi-system cases; outcomes in the absence of available physician consultation; performance in states with full practice authority vs. supervised states (rarely compared directly)

**Is the measured variable direct evidence of the claimed construct?**
No. It is a proxy, and a distant one. The construct is *independent practice equivalence across full scope*. The measurement is *process compliance on chronic disease metrics in supervised settings*.

## 4. LEVEL-OF-ANALYSIS CHECK

**Evidence collected at:** Collaborative/supervised outpatient primary care; stable chronic disease panels; administrative outcome metrics

**Claim applied at:** Independent unsupervised practice; full undifferentiated scope including acute, complex, multi-system, and diagnostically ambiguous presentations; controlled substance prescribing without oversight

**Assumptions required to bridge the levels:**

1. That performance on chronic disease maintenance predicts performance on novel diagnostic reasoning
1. That the ability to escalate to a supervising physician (present in the study setting) is irrelevant to outcomes
1. That 500 hours of clinical exposure produces the same diagnostic pattern-recognition as 12,000–16,000 hours
1. That case-mix in collaborative settings is equivalent to case-mix in fully independent settings

**Are those assumptions tested?** No. None of them. They are not even acknowledged in most source papers as assumptions requiring justification.

**Would a domain expert accept the bridge?** No practicing internist, hospitalist, or emergency physician would accept the claim that chronic disease metric management in a supervised setting validates independent practice across undifferentiated acute illness. The gap is immediately obvious to any clinician familiar with how diagnostic reasoning develops and how collaborative settings filter case complexity.

## 6. ECOLOGICAL VALIDITY CHECK

- **Does the task resemble the real-world phenomenon?** No. The studies measure NPs in settings with physician backup. The policy being advocated removes physician backup. These are not the same clinical environment.
- **Does the sample resemble the population the claim is applied to?** No. The study populations are stable chronic disease patients. The claim covers the full scope of undifferentiated primary care.
- **Does the measured behavior occur under realistic pressure and constraints?** No. The critical constraint being removed — physician supervision — is present in every major outcome study used to justify removing it.
- **Is performance, compliance, or institutional output being conflated?** Yes. Process compliance on chronic disease metrics is being labeled as “equivalent clinical performance.”

**Verdict: Not ecologically valid for the claim being made.** Ecologically valid only for the narrow claim that NPs in collaborative settings can manage stable chronic conditions comparably to physicians managing stable chronic conditions.

-----

## 7. DECISIVE-TEST CHECK

**What the decisive test would require:**

- NPs practicing independently (no physician backup available) vs. physicians practicing independently
- On identical, blinded, undifferentiated patient panels including acute, complex, multi-system, and rare presentations
- Outcomes: diagnostic accuracy, time-to-correct-diagnosis, adverse events, missed diagnosis rates, mortality — not satisfaction scores or process compliance
- In states with full practice authority, with adequate case-mix adjustment
- Prospective design, not administrative data extraction

**Does the current literature run this test?** No. No study in the NP equivalence literature runs this test. The closest approximations (state-level comparisons after full practice authority laws) have serious methodological weaknesses including self-selection of NPs into independent practice, unmeasured case-mix differences, and short follow-up.

**Does the field acknowledge the decisive test has not been run?** Rarely, in the advocacy literature. Some academic researchers note the limitation; it does not appear in legislative testimony or AANP position papers.

**The decisive test would be:** A prospective, case-mix-controlled comparison of diagnostic accuracy and adverse outcome rates between independently practicing NPs and physicians on identical undifferentiated patient panels, in the absence of physician consultation availability.

-----

## 8. CITATION-USE AUDIT

**How the source literature is used downstream:**

- Legislative testimony cites “studies show NPs provide equivalent care” without specifying that the studies examined supervised settings
- AANP position papers cite the outcome literature as if it validated independent practice, omitting the collaborative-setting confound in every instance reviewed
- State legislative analyses cite advocacy summaries of academic papers rather than the papers themselves — a two-step laundering
- The Bloomberg investigation is cited by critics; AANP responded by attacking the journalism rather than addressing the methodological substance, which is itself a citation-avoidance move

**Downstream classification:**

- Academic citations within the field: **Slightly to Major inflation** — authors frequently cite predecessors’ equivalence claims while replicating the same confounded design
- AANP and advocacy use: **Citation laundering** — the collaborative-setting limitation is structurally removed from the conclusion
- Legislative testimony: **Premise laundering** — the equivalence finding is treated as an established fact requiring no methodological scrutiny, then used as the premise for scope-of-practice expansion

-----

## 11. VERDICT

**E. Fatal construct-validity failure for the claim being made**, with elements of F (citation-laundered premise).

The studies measure process compliance on chronic disease metrics among NPs operating in collaborative settings with physician backup available. The claim being advanced — that this evidence justifies independent unsupervised practice across the full scope of primary care — requires bridging at least four untested assumptions: that supervised performance predicts unsupervised performance, that chronic disease management competence predicts diagnostic reasoning in undifferentiated presentations, that case-mix is equivalent between study settings and independent practice settings, and that 500 unverified clinical hours produces equivalent diagnostic pattern recognition to 12,000–16,000 structured hours. None of these assumptions are tested. The most critical confound — that physician backup is present in every major study and absent in the policy being advocated — is structurally ignored rather than acknowledged. The result is that the literature’s conclusions are not wrong about what was measured; they are wrong about what was proven.

-----

## 12. PAPER-SAFE WORDING

**A. Neutral summary sentence:**
Existing NP outcome studies find comparable chronic disease management metrics between NPs and physicians in collaborative practice settings with physician oversight available.

**B. Direct methodological critique:**
The equivalence literature measures NP performance in supervised settings, then uses those findings to justify removing supervision — a level-of-analysis error that the studies themselves do not resolve.

**C. Strong adversarial version:**
Every major study cited to justify NP independent practice authority examined NPs who had physician backup available; the claim that this evidence validates removing physician backup is not a modest extrapolation, it is a direct inversion of the study condition.

**D. Decisive-test sentence:**
The question of NP independent practice equivalence can only be answered by a prospective comparison of diagnostic accuracy and adverse outcomes between NPs and physicians managing identical undifferentiated patient panels without physician consultation available — a study that has not been conducted.

-----

## 13. FAILURE-MODE CLASSIFICATION

- [x] **Untested premise** — That 500 clinical hours produces the same diagnostic competence as 12,000–16,000 hours. Asserted; never tested directly.
- [x] **Proxy problem** — Chronic disease process metrics proxy for clinical judgment in undifferentiated presentations. The proxy distance is large.
- [x] **Fatal proxy failure** — Patient satisfaction scores are used as evidence of diagnostic competence. These are orthogonal constructs.
- [x] **Level-of-analysis error** — Evidence from supervised settings is applied to justify unsupervised settings. This is the central structural failure of the literature.
- [x] **Ecological-validity failure** — The study environment (physician backup available) is precisely the condition being removed by the policy the studies are used to support.
- [x] **Omitted confound** — Case-mix selection, escalation availability, and training hour verification are uncontrolled and unacknowledged in most studies.
- [x] **Citation laundering** — Advocacy literature cites academic papers while removing the collaborative-setting limitation from the conclusion.
- [x] **Premise laundering** — Legislative testimony treats equivalence as an established premise requiring no scrutiny.
- [x] **Ignored adjacent literature** — Diagnostic reasoning development, deliberate practice, and missed-diagnosis epidemiology are absent from the advocacy literature entirely.
- [x] **Missing decisive test** — The study that would actually answer the question (independent NPs vs. physicians on undifferentiated panels, no backup) has not been run.
- [ ] Intervention ratchet — Not the primary failure mode here, though 26-state legislative adoption creates a de facto ratchet effect.
- [x] **Institutional measurement gap** — Training hour verification is structurally absent; the Bloomberg findings document this at scale.
- [ ] Motive claim unsupported
- [x] **Motive claim supported by public record / policy architecture** — AANP’s response to Bloomberg (attacking the journalism, not the substance) and the architecture of advocacy-funded research programs are consistent with institutional interest distorting evidentiary standards. This is not a motive inference; it is a pattern documented in the public record.

## 14. FINAL OUTPUT

|Field                                |Assessment                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
|-------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Strongest defensible claim**       |NPs in collaborative settings with physician backup produce comparable chronic disease management metrics on a limited set of process measures in stable patient panels                                                                                                                           |
|**Claim being overextended**         |That this finding justifies independent unsupervised practice across the full undifferentiated scope of primary care                                                                                                                                                                              |
|**Main proxy problem**               |Chronic disease process compliance metrics do not measure diagnostic reasoning in novel, complex, or high-acuity presentations                                                                                                                                                                    |
|**Main level-of-analysis problem**   |All major studies measure supervised NPs; the policy removes supervision — the study condition and the policy condition are structurally opposite                                                                                                                                                 |
|**Main omitted confound**            |Physician backup availability during the study allows escalation that will not be available under full practice authority                                                                                                                                                                         |
|**Decisive test**                    |Prospective, case-mix-controlled comparison of NP vs. physician diagnostic accuracy on undifferentiated panels without physician consultation available                                                                                                                                           |
|**Citation-use verdict**             |Citation laundering and premise laundering; the collaborative-setting limitation is systematically removed as the finding moves from academic paper to advocacy document to legislative testimony                                                                                                 |
|**Overall verdict**                  |Fatal construct-validity failure for the independent practice claim; partial evidence only for the narrow supervised-setting chronic-disease claim                                                                                                                                                |
|**Confidence level**                 |High — the level-of-analysis error is not contested even within the field; it is simply not disclosed in advocacy contexts                                                                                                                                                                        |
|**What would change this assessment**|A well-powered prospective study comparing NPs and physicians in independent practice on matched undifferentiated patient panels with hard outcomes (diagnostic accuracy, adverse events, mortality) — if equivalence held under those conditions, the strong claim would have evidentiary support|

-----

*Note: The practitioner frustration and investigative journalism flagged in the source framing are valid discovery tools — the Bloomberg findings on unverified hours and program proliferation are signals, not conclusions. This audit treats them as corroborating context for the structural measurement failures, not as independent proof. The receipts are in the level-of-analysis error, which is documentable from the source papers themselves without reliance on the adjacent reporting.*
