CHANGE 1 — “Honest concession” label removed (Claim 3)

ORIGINAL:
“Honest concession: This is the strongest of the five claims. Water stress in arid regions
during drought years is a real documented conflict. Local governments in water-stressed
communities have legitimate concerns about allocation.”

PROBLEM:
The “Honest concession” label is compliance performance — the author audibly flagging
their own good faith before making a methodological point. A confident author does not
announce the concession; they make it. The substance was doing real evidentiary work.
The label was not.

VOICE-RESTORED:
“The water consumption figures are real. Hyperscale facilities in arid regions do create
documented stress during drought years, and local governments in those communities have
legitimate allocation concerns. The failure is in the jump from local stress to national
catastrophe: the denominator was omitted entirely. ‘Towns go dry’ is the conclusion you
reach when you report the numerator and erase the denominator.”

CLASSIFICATION: Compliance performance / self-announced good faith
ACTION: Rewrite — drop label, keep substance, add the denominator punch line

CHANGE 2 — Repeated “costs exist and deserve honest mitigation” line cut
(What the Posters Don’t Count section)

ORIGINAL (second instance):
“The posters present a zero-sum worldview: data centers = pure loss for regular people.
The actual dynamic is investment with real but solvable trade-offs. The costs exist and
deserve honest mitigation. The benefits — jobs, tax revenue, and massive productivity
gains — are being deliberately erased from the conversation.”

PROBLEM:
“The costs exist and deserve honest mitigation” appears verbatim in the Political Version
paragraph and again here. The second instance, combined with the “some communities
may rationally decide” paragraph immediately following, creates a back-to-back cluster of
reassurances at the end of the section’s strongest argumentative block. The “some
communities” paragraph is doing legitimate scope work on its own. The repeated line adds
no new information — it only softens the momentum before the close.

VOICE-RESTORED:
“The posters present a zero-sum worldview: data centers = pure loss for regular people.
The actual dynamic is investment with real but solvable trade-offs — and the benefit side
of that ledger is being deliberately erased from the conversation.”

CLASSIFICATION: Repeated reassurance / redundant good-faith signal
ACTION: Cut the repeated line; retain the “some communities” paragraph as written
