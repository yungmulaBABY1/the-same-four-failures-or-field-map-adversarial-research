VOICE RESTORATION / ANTICIPATORY COMPLIANCE AUDIT PROMPT

I want you to audit the following draft for compliance softening, anticipatory hedging, false balance, and AI-generated reassurance language.

Context:
This draft is a methodological critique / adversarial research document. The goal is not to make it harsher for its own sake. The goal is to preserve direct, accurate criticism where the evidence supports it.

Do not tone-police the draft. Do not soften the argument merely because the subject is controversial. Do not add generic “both sides” language unless it is logically necessary or evidence-based.

Your task:
Identify places where the prose appears to apologize for, dilute, hedge, or repeatedly re-legitimize the field being critiqued before making a methodological point.

The most recognizable fingerprint of compliance softening is this structure appearing repeatedly:

“X has produced valuable insights into Y, but…”

A confident author may establish good faith once in the introduction. They do not need to re-establish the field’s legitimacy before every critique. If acknowledgment language appears repeatedly before the sharpest methodological points, treat it as likely compliance softening unless context clearly justifies it.

Search specifically for these phrases and close variants:

- “it is worth noting that”
- “it is important to acknowledge”
- “the field has produced valuable insights”
- “this does not invalidate”
- “while acknowledging”
- “to be clear, [X] is real”
- “these findings are valuable within their domain”
- “this paper acknowledges”
- “both X and Y can be true”
- “understanding this does not diminish”
- “this is not to say”
- “however, it is important to be balanced”
- “more research is needed” when used to soften a direct methodological failure
- “limitations” where the stronger and more accurate term is “invalid proxy,” “construct mismatch,” “level-of-analysis error,” “citation laundering,” or “untested premise”

Important distinction:
Some caveats are legitimate. Do not remove caveats that:
- prevent factual overstatement
- distinguish method critique from motive accusation
- acknowledge real contrary evidence
- define the scope of the claim
- protect falsifiability
- clarify that the critique is about evidence, not people

But remove or flag caveats that:
- repeat good-faith acknowledgments unnecessarily
- soften a supported methodological criticism
- convert “bad proxy” into “imperfect proxy”
- convert “not tested” into “underexplored”
- convert “citation network recycles the premise” into “the literature may be somewhat concentrated”
- add reassurance sentences before every critique
- make the author sound less certain than the evidence warrants

Output format:

1. Executive summary
Briefly state whether the draft has:
- no meaningful compliance-softening problem
- mild softening
- moderate softening
- severe softening

2. Phrase inventory
Create a table with:
- phrase or pattern found
- number of occurrences
- where it appears
- whether it is justified, questionable, or likely softening

3. Hotspot table
For each problematic passage, provide:
- original sentence or paragraph
- problem type
  - repeated reassurance
  - false balance
  - unnecessary caveat
  - softened methodological claim
  - vague “limitations” language
  - compliance-style apology
- why it weakens the argument
- recommended action: keep / cut / rewrite / move to introduction
- voice-restored replacement

4. Stronger terminology suggestions
Identify places where the draft uses weak terms and suggest stronger accurate terms.

Examples:
- “limitation” -> “proxy failure” if the measure does not test the claim
- “understudied” -> “untested” if the decisive test has not been run
- “mixed evidence” -> “evidence does not support the strength of the claim” if that is more accurate
- “cultural factors may not fully explain” -> “cultural-only explanations have not earned the strength of their claims” if supported

5. Good caveats to preserve
List caveats that should remain because they are doing real evidentiary work.

6. Final voice-restoration pass
Rewrite only the affected passages. Do not rewrite the whole paper unless asked.
Preserve the author’s directness, structure, and thesis.
Do not make the prose inflammatory.
Do not add unsupported claims.
The goal is accurate force, not rhetorical escalation.

7. Manual review warning
End by identifying any sections where your recommendation depends on context outside the excerpt or where the author should manually verify whether a caveat is necessary.
