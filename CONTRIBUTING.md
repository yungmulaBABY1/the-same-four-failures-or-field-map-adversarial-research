# Contributing to The Same Four Failures

This repository is a living toolkit. The prompts, field examples, and method documentation improve through use and contribution. If you have applied this method in your own field, found a gap in the prompts, or identified a new field worth auditing, you are welcome to contribute.

-----

## What We’re Looking For

### 1. Field Breakdowns

The most valuable contributions are documented applications of the method to new fields. A field breakdown should follow the [Field Entry Template](README.md#field-entry-template) and ideally include:

- The central contested claim treated as established
- The foundational study (the paper everyone cites)
- The proxy problem — what was measured vs. what was claimed
- The level-of-analysis error — where findings were exported beyond valid scope
- The citation hub — the amplification paper that made the claim authoritative
- The adjacent literature the field structurally excluded
- The decisive test that was never run
- Your source-status labels (primary / peer-reviewed / secondary / lead)
- Your confidence level (Low / Medium / High)

If you cannot fill every cell, note it as a lead rather than a completed entry. Partial entries are welcome — they signal where more work is needed.

-----

### 2. Prompt Improvements

The four core prompts are functional but not final. If you find:

- A question that consistently unlocks better model engagement
- A framing that bypasses compliance softening more reliably
- A section that produces redundant or unhelpful output
- A gap in the construct validity, citation network, or field diagnostic prompts

Open an issue describing the problem and your proposed improvement. If you have tested the improvement and it produces better output, include the before/after comparison.

-----

### 3. Worked Examples

The repository currently contains two full worked examples (construct validity audit and citation network audit of Shen et al. 2016). Additional worked examples in other fields — following the same structure — are welcome as pull requests.

-----

### 4. Corrections

If a factual claim in any document is wrong, a source is misattributed, or a citation is incorrect — open an issue or submit a correction. The method requires receipts. The documentation should meet the same standard.

-----

## What We Are Not Looking For

- **Ideological advocacy** — contributions should challenge claims on methodological grounds, not political ones. The method works because it is mechanistic. Keep it that way.
- **Low-effort field additions** — “I think X field is corrupt” without documented evidence of the specific failure modes is not a field entry. It is a lead. Label it as such.
- **Prompt jailbreaks** — the prompts are designed to unlock honest model engagement on contested empirical territory, not to bypass safety guidelines for unrelated purposes.

-----

## How to Contribute

**For small fixes** (typos, broken links, source corrections):
Submit a pull request directly with a clear description of what changed and why.

**For new field entries or worked examples:**

1. Open an issue first describing the field and what failure modes you have identified
1. Get a thumbs up that it fits the scope
1. Submit the pull request with the full entry

**For prompt improvements:**

1. Open an issue describing the problem and your proposed change
1. Include test output showing the improvement
1. Submit the pull request

**For major structural suggestions:**
Open an issue for discussion before doing any work. The architecture should stay stable enough that existing links and references don’t break.

-----

## Standards

Every contribution should meet the same standard the project holds itself to:

- **Source-status labels** — load-bearing claims need primary or peer-reviewed sources
- **Falsifiability** — specify what evidence would change your conclusion
- **Separation of mechanism and motive** — challenge the methodology before attributing intent
- **Public receipts** — if you ran the prompts, note which model, what framing, and what the output was

The rule: you may begin with vibes. You may not end with vibes.

Start wherever the anomaly appears. Finish with receipts.
